# Cursor Acquires Graphite (December 2025)

## The deal
- Announced December 19, 2025. Cursor (Anysphere) signed a definitive agreement to acquire Graphite, the code review platform.
- Terms undisclosed, but the deal was a mix of cash and equity, and reporting indicated Cursor paid well above Graphite's last valuation of $290M (set at its $52M Series B in March 2025).
- Timing: one month after Cursor announced $1B in annualized revenue. Cursor was valued around $29B at the time.
- The entire Graphite team joined Cursor on close. Graphite continues to operate as an independent product and brand.
- This was Cursor's third notable acquisition, after Supermaven (Nov 2024) and the Koala talent acquisition (July 2025).

## Why the deal happened (the core thesis)
- Cursor CEO Michael Truell's framing: over 2.5 years Cursor made writing production code dramatically faster, but for most teams code review looks the same as it did three years ago. Review is becoming the bottleneck as writing time shrinks.
- Graphite CEO Merrill Lutsky's framing: the constraint used to be how fast teams could write code; now it's how fast they can review it. Graphite's long-term vision is one integrated platform where humans and agents create, review, and merge code collaboratively, with PRs becoming effectively self-driving.
- Both companies shared investors (Accel, a16z, Neo) and largest customers, and Truell knew the Graphite founders (Merrill Lutsky, Greg Foster, Tomas Reimers) from before Cursor existed.

## Stated integration roadmap
- Double down on Graphite's stacked PRs platform and merge queue.
- Ship integrations connecting local development in Cursor, background agents, and pull requests. Cursor Cloud Agents are available inside Graphite, so teams can create, review, and ship without leaving the PR.
- Combine Graphite's AI Reviewer with Cursor's Bugbot into a single, stronger AI reviewer.
- "Lay the foundations for a bolder product vision" that was teased at acquisition time. That bolder vision turned out to be Origin.
