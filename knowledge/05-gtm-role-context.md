# GTM Emerging Products Role and Team Context

## The role
- Title: GTM, Emerging Products. Sits on the Origin Incubation team at Cursor.
- Mission: 0-to-1 go-to-market for Origin ahead of and through its fall 2026 general release. Customer discovery, validating what enterprises will pay for, building the first sales playbook, and feeding market learnings back into product.
- Compensation structure is MBO-based rather than a traditional quota, reflecting the learning-stage nature of the product.
- Explicitly not a traditional AE seat. The application asks candidates why they want this over a standard AE role. The right answer centers on wanting to discover why a product wins rather than execute someone else's playbook.
- Location: San Francisco or New York, 3-4 days in office.

## The team
- Origin Incubation is led by Carissa Jansen (MIT, based in New York, LinkedIn headline "Building Origin at Cursor"). She came over with the Graphite acquisition.
- The GTM leadership includes the operator who took Graphite from $0 to $17MM ARR as Head of Sales & Operations, now in GTM Leadership at Cursor. Before that, they were Graphite's first business hire.
- Culture markers from the Graphite founders' own words: talent density, urgency, and craft.

## What this team has already proven
- They built a $0 to $17MM ARR motion selling a developer-workflow product (Graphite) into elite engineering orgs.
- They know PLG-to-enterprise mechanics: bottom-up developer love converted into top-down platform deals.
- For Origin they must partially relearn: hosting is a bigger, stickier, more security-sensitive purchase than a review tool. Migration risk, compliance, and procurement will loom larger.

## What they likely need in this hire
- Someone technical enough to run credible discovery with platform engineering leaders and demo an API/MCP-extensible product.
- Someone comfortable with ambiguity: no playbook, no marketing air cover, MBO goals that shift as learning accumulates.
- Someone who ships: bias to action, builds their own demos and collateral, treats the product as a daily tool rather than a slide.
